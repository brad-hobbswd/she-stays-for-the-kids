Chapter 5: The Lead

She stands in the room as the one responsible.

The title carries weight before any action takes place. Expectations attach themselves to it automatically. Direction, organization, awareness. The role suggests that all of these should be present, steady, and consistent.

The reality does not always match what the role requires.

She moves carefully through the space, navigating more than what others see at a glance. Sound does not always reach her in the way it reaches others. Details are not always clear from a distance. The room requires constant adjustment, and she meets it with effort that is not always visible.

She stays close to what she can control.

Surfaces are cleaned. Materials are handled. Tasks that can be completed in front of her are done with focus. These actions create a sense of order, even when the larger structure of the room is not fully aligned.

Communication does not always come easily.

Moments pass where direction could be shared, though nothing is said. Not out of refusal, but out of uncertainty. What should be communicated is not always clear in the moment, and when it is missed, the opportunity does not return the same way.

When something is brought to her attention, the response is often simple.

“I wasn’t told.”

The words are not defensive. They carry confusion more than resistance. They reflect a gap that she feels, even if she cannot always define it.

Responsibility remains, even within that gap.

Paperwork waits to be completed. Expectations sit in place whether they are fully understood or not. Attempts are made to follow through, though not always successfully. The repetition of being shown does not always lead to retention. Each attempt feels new, even when it has been done before.

The room continues around her, moving faster than she can always match.

Children approach without warning. Movement shifts quickly. Space closes in before there is time to adjust. There have been moments when the distance between her and the children has led to reactions she did not intend to cause. The result is not only disruption, but injury.

Time away from the room has followed.

Returning does not reset what happened. It only places her back into the same environment, with the same expectations, and the same pace.

She has spoken about it plainly.

There are moments when she does not believe she fits the role she has been given. The words come without performance, without an attempt to convince anyone otherwise.

Still, she remains.

Her reason does not change.

She stays for the kids.

The statement is not meant to justify the struggle. It reflects a connection that exists even within it. The children are familiar to her. Their presence is consistent in a way that the structure of the room is not.

Others may see what is missing. She feels what is present.

Effort continues, even when outcomes do not improve. Movement continues, even when direction is not fully established. The role remains hers, whether or not it is fully supported.

The room does not adjust to meet her.

She continues trying to meet the room.

Between what is expected and what is possible, she holds her place as best as she can.

Not because it is easy.

Because leaving would mean letting go of the one part that still feels certain.

She stayed for the kids.
