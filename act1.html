Act I

Entering the Room

The beginning does not announce itself. Nothing signals that this room will be different from any other. At first glance, everything appears familiar. A classroom. A schedule. A group of children ready to move through the day.

What unfolds is not immediate. The shift happens gradually, through missed moments and quiet inconsistencies. Directions are given, though not always followed. Transitions begin, though not always completed. The rhythm of the day never fully settles into place.

Those who enter believe structure will come with effort. Experience suggests that consistency will guide the room into order. The expectation feels reasonable. The reality proves more complex.

Act I introduces the environment as it is, not as it was intended to be. It reveals the first signs of imbalance and the early moments where control begins to slip.
