Chapter 7: Removed

The changes do not come with warning. They arrive quietly, and then they are simply there.

One day, a person is part of the room. The next, they are not.

The absence is noticed in small ways at first. A voice that is no longer heard. A presence that no longer moves through the space. The work remains, though the person who carried part of it does not.

An assistant is gone.

The reasons are known, though not discussed in the room itself. Long breaks. Moments where exhaustion became visible. A level of strain that could no longer be overlooked. The decision is made elsewhere, and the result is placed into the day without explanation.

The room adjusts, though not by choice.

Responsibilities shift without direction. What was already uneven becomes more so. Those who remain take on what has been left behind, whether they are prepared for it or not.

The pattern continues.

There had been another lead before the current one.

That position had also been filled, and then emptied. The role remained, though the person did not. What was left behind was not structure, but a space that had never fully settled before changing again.

The room does not reset when someone leaves.

It carries forward everything that was unresolved.

A new assistant steps in.

She arrives ready. Prepared. Certain that effort and consistency will create the difference that has been missing. The belief is familiar. It has been carried into the room before.

At first, she moves with confidence.

Directions are clear. Energy is steady. The intention to bring order is present in everything she does. The room responds in brief moments, just enough to suggest that change is possible.

Those moments do not hold.

What begins as effort meets the same gaps that existed before she arrived. Instructions that are not carried through. Communication that does not connect. Expectations that do not remain consistent between those responsible for them.

She notices it gradually.

A shift in how long she stands before sitting. A pause before stepping in. A quiet moment where suggestion is replaced with observation.

The change is not announced.

It is absorbed.

The room continues in the same pattern, though now with different people moving through it. The names change. The faces change. The outcome does not.

Some leave on their own.

Some are removed.

The difference does not alter what remains.

There is word of another lead who is expected to return.

She is not in the room yet. She is being trained somewhere else, away from the environment she will eventually step into. There are already signs that the weight of the role may be more than expected. The possibility of leaving exists before fully arriving.

Still, the expectation is that she will come back.

Back to what has not changed.

The room continues to move forward, though not toward resolution. Each removal creates space, though not stability. Each new arrival brings effort, though not lasting change.

The pattern does not break.

It repeats.

Those who remain feel it most clearly.

Not in one moment, but in the accumulation of all of them. The understanding that what happens is not isolated. It is part of something that continues, regardless of who is present.

The room does not hold people.

It cycles them.
